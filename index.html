Understand your duty

Who is responsible for workplace safety?

Responsibilities extend well beyond directors and business owners. Managers, supervisors, and anyone who influences work activities also carry duties under Australian WHS legislation.

  • Site managers can hold responsibility
  • Operations managers may be accountable
  • Supervisors directing work must ensure safety
  • Responsibilities apply regardless of business size
  • Lack of awareness does not remove legal duty

Why this matters

If an incident occurs, investigators assess whether reasonable steps were taken to identify and manage risks. Clear assessments, documented procedures and visible safety management matter.

That's why many businesses choose to proactively assess their workplace and address gaps before an incident occurs.

Talk to a consultant
Do I legally need a workplace safety assessment?

There's no single rule that names an "assessment", but WHS legislation requires you to identify hazards and manage risks so far as is reasonably practicable. A structured assessment is the most reliable way to demonstrate you've taken those reasonable steps.

How often should workplace safety systems be reviewed?

At minimum annually, and whenever something changes — new equipment, new processes, incidents, or regulatory updates. Ongoing review keeps your systems current and defensible.

Can managers be personally liable for safety breaches?

Yes. Duties under WHS law extend to managers, supervisors and anyone who influences work activities — regardless of business size. Lack of awareness does not remove the legal duty.

What happens during a safety audit?

We review your workplace against relevant obligations, identify compliance gaps and risk exposure, then provide clear, prioritised implementation guidance — not a generic checklist.

Do you provide ongoing support after an assessment?

Yes. We provide continued guidance, follow-up support and long-term compliance assistance, backed by Your Safety Portal for day-to-day management.

Is Your Safety Portal mandatory?

No. The Portal is there to support the advice and implementation we provide. Many clients find it invaluable for keeping systems active, but it's optional.
